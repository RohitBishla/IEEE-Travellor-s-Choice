New Delhi

About New Delhi Places To Visit Stay

Dive into History

Laid out by British architect Edwin Lutyens, the Indian capital is a striking modern metropolis. A gracious contrast to Old Delhi's winding streets, the grand avenues and stately buildings of New Delhi are rich with history and culture, from Gandhi's Delhi home (and the site of his assassination) to the tomb of Humayun, a complex of Mughal buildings reminiscent of the Taj Mahal. Chaotic traffic is best left to the locals. Negotiate a good price for taxis or travel on the new Delhi Metro.

Swaminarayan Akshardham

It is one man's creation in five years totally with voluntary support. A monument which makes every indian proud!

Red Fort

This 17th century fort was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan and served as the capital of the Mughals until 1857.

Rashtrapati Bhavan

The 200,000-square-foot palace, completed in 1929, is the largest residence of any head of the state in the world.

Sunder Nursery Park

NATURE. HERITAGE. CULTURE. LAKES. GARDENS. Abutting Humayun's Tomb, SUNDER NURSERY, is Delhi's finest Heritage Park.
